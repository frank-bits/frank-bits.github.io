The best recipe keeper app you can try free

Updated July 2026 · about 4 min read

Recipe apps ask for a lot up front — sometimes payment before you've saved a single recipe, or a signup just to see whether the thing even fits how you cook. If all you want is a tidy place to keep the recipes you already love, it's fair to want to try before you commit.

Here's what actually matters when you're choosing a recipe keeper — and why being able to try one free first makes the decision easy.

What to look for in a recipe keeper

1. It saves recipes from where they already live

Your recipes are in screenshots, photos of cookbook pages, and web links. A good recipe keeper imports from all of those without making you retype anything. If import is the feature they charge for, that's the one that hurts most.

2. Organizing is included, not a premium tier

Tags, categories, and search are the whole point of a recipe organizer. Be wary of apps that cap how many recipes you can save for free, or hide search behind a paywall.

3. Meal planning and grocery lists come along

Keeping recipes is step one; cooking from them is the payoff. A planner that turns saved recipes into a grocery list — without a separate purchase — is worth prioritizing.

4. You can try it before you pay

You shouldn't have to commit sight-unseen. Look for an app that's free to download and try, so you can import a few recipes and see whether it fits how you cook before you decide to upgrade.

SavorKeep is a recipe keeper for iPhone that's free to download and try. It saves recipes from screenshots, photos, and websites, organizes them into your own searchable cookbook, and turns them into meal plans and grocery lists — so you can see how it fits your kitchen before you commit.
