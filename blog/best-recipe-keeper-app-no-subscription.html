The best recipe keeper app without a subscription

Updated July 2026 · about 4 min read

Recipe apps have quietly gone the way of everything else: a monthly fee to keep your own recipes. Want to sync across devices, import from a website, or save more than a handful of recipes? That'll be a subscription. If all you want is a tidy place to keep the recipes you already love, that can feel like a lot.

You don't always need to pay monthly for a good recipe keeper. Here's what actually matters when you're choosing one — and how to avoid the subscription trap.

What to look for in a subscription-free recipe keeper

1. It saves recipes from where they already live

Your recipes are in screenshots, photos of cookbook pages, and web links. A good recipe keeper imports from all of those without making you retype anything. If import is the feature they charge for, that's the one that hurts most.

2. Organizing is included, not a premium tier

Tags, categories, and search are the whole point of a recipe organizer. Be wary of apps that cap how many recipes you can save for free, or hide search behind a paywall.

3. Meal planning and grocery lists come along

Keeping recipes is step one; cooking from them is the payoff. A planner that turns saved recipes into a grocery list — without a separate purchase — is worth prioritizing.

4. Your recipes stay yours

You should be able to keep and access your collection without a recurring charge holding it hostage. Look for apps that don't lock your existing recipes the moment a trial ends.

A free recipe keeper that checks the boxes

SavorKeep is a recipe keeper for iPhone that's free to download. It saves recipes from screenshots, photos, and websites, organizes them into your own searchable cookbook, and turns them into meal plans and grocery lists — no subscription required to keep and organize your recipes.

The honest trade-off

Subscription apps like the well-known pro recipe managers are genuinely powerful, and if you live in a recipe app all day they can be worth it. But most home cooks don't need a pro suite — they need their screenshots to stop disappearing. If that's you, a free recipe keeper that nails capture, organization, and meal planning is the better fit.
